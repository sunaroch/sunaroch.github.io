About Me

Welcome to my website! My name is Sun Aroch, and I am a New York City based programmer and musician. I recently graduated from Barnard College of Columbia University with a degree in computer science, and I am constantly exploring the intersections between technology and the arts. The arts have always been an integral part of my life, as I have been playing instruments and producing music since I was a child. I also thrive in the world of programming, with experience in languages such as C++, Python, Java, and HTML. This unique blend of technical skills and artistic sensibilities allows me to create captivating and innovative experiences.

email: arochsun@gmail.com

Portfolio

  • Sun Synth - This is a project where I explored the connections between an ESP32 and MaxMSP. I created a small instrument titled "Sun Synth" where i outputed the values from the ESP32 into the Arduino IDE, then used OSC messages to create a Max patch in order sonify them.
  • Face to Video - In this project, I experimented with facial recognition through MaxMSP and its interaction with audio and video. I explored how feelings of connection can be visualized and sonified, with different facial movements leading to different effects.
  • Detroit Techno Meets Live Coding - A track that I created using Sonic Pi, Logic Pro X, and inspiration from the genre of Detroit Techno.
  • An Exploration of Improvisation - My peer, Lili, and I collaborated on a final project for our class: Computational Sound. We decided to explore the concept of automated composition and improvisation using magenta.js and cellular automata.
